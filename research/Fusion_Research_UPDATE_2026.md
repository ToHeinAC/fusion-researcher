# Fusion Energy Research Update
## binding.energy 2026 Conference (Aachen, Feb 3-5, 2026)

### Executive Summary

This update integrates first-hand information from the binding.energy 2026 conference in Aachen with current market data. The fusion and SMR sector shows unprecedented momentum with major funding rounds, government programs, and supply chain development.

---

## 1. German Fusion Ecosystem

### 1.1 Funding Program "Fusion 2040"

The **BMFTR (Bundesministerium für Forschung, Technologie und Raumfahrt)** has established:
- **€500 million** initial funding for fusion research
- **Three National Fusion Hubs** planned:
  1. Magnetic Fusion Hub
  2. Laser Fusion Hub  
  3. Fuel Cycle & Materials Hub
- **Fusion Action Plan** by end 2025
- **FIRE Roadmap** (Forschungs- und Innovationsroadmap FusionsEnergie) by 2026

Key elements from the Hightech Agenda Deutschland:
- Fusion identified as one of six critical technologies
- Goal: Make Germany a leading innovation hub for fusion technologies
- Target: German companies as global market leaders

### 1.2 Site Development

**Biblis Site (Hessen)**
- Former NPP, now designated as **Laser Fusion Hub**
- MoU partners: RWE, Land Hessen, Focused Energy, TU Darmstadt, SCHOTT, Heraeus, Arthur D. Little
- €20 million initial research support from Hessen
- Target: 1 GW fusion power plant by 2035
- Estimated cost: €5-7 billion
- 3D scan of ZT building complex completed January 2026

**Gundremmingen Site (Bayern)**
- Bayern announced reactor plans at Forum Fusion 2026
- Potential partners: Proxima Fusion, Transmutex
- Competition between states for fusion hubs intensifying

## 4. Transmutation Technologies

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Funding | CHF 20 million (Series A2) |
| Founded | 2019 (CERN spin-out) |

## 6. Regulatory & Safety Framework

### 6.1 German Regulatory Landscape

**ReFus Project** (GRS, MPI Public Laws, TÜV Rheinland, Öko Institut):
- Fusion facilities do NOT require Atomic Energy Act license
- Licensed under Radiation Protection Act
- No environmental impact assessment required
- ALARA principle per ICRP 103 (2007)

### 6.2 Key Standards

- ISO 19443: Framework for implementing new nuclear technologies
- DIN 25422: Storage standards
- Digital 3D Models for lifecycle coverage
